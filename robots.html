Our Robots Through the Years

2024–2025: Into the Deep

This year’s challenge takes us underwater in “Into the Deep.” Our robot features an arm extension and object sorter designed to maneuver and place objects in simulated underwater environments. This season brings major mechanical improvements and a refined control system.

2023–2024: Centerstage

Our robot focused on precision placement of pixels and backdrop scoring. It used a linear slide system and dual intake for effective cycle times. This season marked our return to the NorCal Championships!

2022–2023: Power Play

For Power Play, we built a turreted arm robot that specialized in cone placement. We iterated heavily on drivetrain tuning, autonomous reliability, and signal sleeve vision.

2021–2022: Freight Frenzy

Our Freight Frenzy robot used mecanum wheels and a linear slide to score freight at multiple levels. We also experimented with active intakes and duck spinners.

2020–2021: Ultimate Goal

Despite COVID, our team developed a ring-shooting robot with a hopper-style magazine and flywheel. It was our first year diving into custom CAD-heavy designs.

2019–2020: Skystone

In Skystone, our team emphasized autonomous precision and block stacking. The robot was one of our first to use a full aluminum frame and active intake system.

2014–2018: Early Years

From our rookie year in 2014 to Rover Ruckus in 2018, our team learned the fundamentals of engineering, programming, and teamwork. These years laid the foundation for our success in future challenges.
