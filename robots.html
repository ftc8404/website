Past Robots

A legacy of innovation. Explore the evolution of our robot design, from our earliest to our latest championship-winning machines.

2024–2025: INTO THE DEEP

Designed for precision and underwater-themed challenges, this robot featured optimized scoring and advanced vision.

Features:

  • Efficient pixel intake and placement
  • Backdrop-scoring linear slides
  • AprilTag autonomous
  • Parallel plate chassis

2023–2024: CENTERSTAGE

Award-winning robot designed for the CENTERSTAGE challenge. Fast, modular, and precise.

Features:

  • Ultra-fast intake and lift
  • Preload pixel placement in < 2s
  • Automatic vision-based auto
  • Odometry localization

2022–2023: POWERPLAY

Compact and efficient, this robot featured a powerful arm with a claw for cone manipulation and stacking.

Features:

  • Custom-made omni drivetrain
  • Team Element placement and detection
  • High-stability lifting

2021–2022: FREIGHT FRENZY

Utilized a powerful intake and an adjustable arm system to manage various freight objects.

Features:

  • 360-degree rotation arm
  • Carousel spinner for ducks
  • Multiple auto routines

2020–2021: ULTIMATE GOAL

Focus on ring intake and shooting system. Features included rapid fire shooter and flexible drivetrain.

2019–2020: SKYSTONE

Skystone placement and foundation movement with linear slides and claw system.

2018–2019: ROVER RUCKUS

Hanging and mineral sorting robot. Used claw and extending arm for scoring.

2017–2018: RELIC RECOVERY

Glyph placement and relic recovery with jewel-knocker and omni chassis.

2016–2017: VELOCITY VORTEX

Ball shooter and cap ball pusher. Autonomous beacons and particle management.

2015–2016: RES-Q

Mountain climber robot with debris sweeper and pull-up mechanism.

2014–2015: CASCADE EFFECT

Game piece collection and ramp scoring robot. Quixilver’s FTC debut season.
