Past Robots

A legacy of innovation. Explore the evolution of our robot design, from our earliest to our latest championship-winning machines.

2014–2015: CASCADE EFFECT

Our rookie season! Focused on precision ball handling and a stable drive base.

Features:

  • Side sweeper intake
  • Rolling goal delivery
  • Simple tank drive

2015–2016: RES-Q

Designed for climbing and debris collection — our first year attempting hanging!

Features:

  • Climbing hook lift
  • Bucket intake
  • Treaded drivetrain

2016–2017: VELOCITY VORTEX

Our first flywheel shooter robot, with beacon pressers and autonomous pathing.

Features:

  • Flywheel particle launcher
  • Beacon color sensors
  • Omnidirectional mecanum drive

2017–2018: RELIC RECOVERY

Focused on glyph stacking and relic extension, with precision and reach.

Features:

  • Dual gripper intake
  • Relic arm with extension
  • Auto balance & alignment

2018–2019: ROVER RUCKUS

Lander hanging, mineral sorting, and one of our first complex auto routines.

Features:

  • 2-stage lift
  • Latch & hang system
  • Team marker dropper

2019–2020: SKYSTONE

Precision block stacking and foundation manipulation with capstone deployment.

Features:

  • Stacking lift
  • Skystone detection
  • Foundation grabbers

2020–2021: ULTIMATE GOAL

A high-speed ring shooter with wobble goal delivery and turret targeting.

Features:

  • Ring shooter & indexer
  • Wobble goal arm
  • Vision assisted aim

2021–2022: FREIGHT FRENZY

Specialized in intake and delivery of freight onto shared hubs.

Features:

  • 4-bar linkage lift
  • Omni intake ramp
  • Duck spinner arm

2022–2023: POWER PLAY

Cone stacking on tall poles with custom claw and linear lift system.

Features:

  • Telescoping lift
  • Auto-align claw
  • Field sensing nav

2023–2024: CENTERSTAGE

Pixel manipulation and drone launch with high reliability and accuracy.

Features:

  • Dual-stage lift
  • Chain-driven intake
  • Drone launcher
